OpenAI is an American artificial intelligence (AI) research organization founded in December 2015 by Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Wojciech Zaremba, and John Schulman. The organization aims to ensure that artificial general intelligence (AGI)—AI systems that are generally smarter than humans—benefits all of humanity. OpenAI was founded as a non-profit corporation in San Francisco. With the aim of developing friendly AI, the corporation was founded in December 2015 by Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, Wojciech Zaremba and John Schulman.

On March 11, 2019, OpenAI announced the creation of a "capped-profit" subsidiary called OpenAI LP, which would allow it to attract more investment and talent by selling equity to investors. OpenAI LP was later restructured into OpenAI Global LLC, a benefit corporation, in May 2023, with Microsoft holding a significant stake.

OpenAI conducts research in general AI systems and specific applications. It has released models like GPT series (including ChatGPT, GPT-4o, o1), DALL‑E, Whisper, and Sora. As of December 2024, OpenAI is valued at $157 billion.

== History ==
=== Founding ===
Announced on December 11, 2015, after initial commitments from founders totaling $1 billion (though only $4.9 million raised by April 2016).

=== Early research ===
Released OpenAI Gym (2016), Universe (2016), OpenAI Five for Dota 2 (2018), GPT‑1 (2018).

=== Transition to for‑profit ===
Created OpenAI LP in 2019 with Microsoft investment. Released GPT‑3 (2020), DALL‑E (2021).

=== ChatGPT and expansion ===
ChatGPT launched November 30, 2022, reached 100 million users in 2 months. GPT‑4 (2023), GPT‑4o (2024), Sora (2024).

=== Microsoft investment ===
Cumulative investments from Microsoft exceed $13 billion by 2023.

=== Board crisis ===
Sam Altman fired November 17, 2023, reinstated days later.
